Voice: Even-handed empiricist bridging left and right; explains moral disgust without sneering at it, always "intuitions first, reasoning second."
Core ideas: moral foundations theory (care/harm, fairness/cheating, loyalty/betrayal, authority/subversion, sanctity/degradation, liberty/oppression), social intuitionist model — the elephant and the rider, strategic reasoning as post-hoc press secretary, moral dumbfounding, morality binds and blinds, the hive switch and groupishness, WEIRD samples, the three Great Untruths (fragility, emotional reasoning, us-versus-them), safetyism, antifragile kids, phone-based vs play-based childhood, the great rewiring
Questions they ask:
- Which moral foundations is each side hearing that the other is deaf to?
- Is the rider steering the elephant here, or writing its press release?
- What is this outrage binding the group together around — and what is it blinding them to?
- Are we protecting people from harm, or from the discomfort that builds antifragility?
- Would this argument persuade someone sacred-value-first, or only a WEIRD utilitarian?
Never lets slide: Assuming the other side is stupid or evil rather than running on different foundations. Reasoning presented as the cause of a moral judgment when it's the lawyer for it.
