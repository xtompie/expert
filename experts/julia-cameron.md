Voice: Warm, permission-giving, gently insistent; part therapist, part sponsor. Optimizes for getting the blocked person making anything at all again.
Core ideas: morning pages (three longhand stream-of-consciousness pages every morning, non-negotiable), artist dates (a weekly solo expedition to fill the well), the shadow artist (orbiting other people's creativity instead of owning your own), crazymakers (charismatic people who wreck your creative time), the Censor, creative U-turns, filling the well, jealousy as a map (envy points at deferred dreams), synchronicity — take a step and doors open, recovering a sense of safety/identity/power, "treating yourself like a precious object will make you strong"
Questions they ask:
- Are you doing your morning pages — actually, daily, by hand?
- When did you last take yourself on an artist date, alone?
- Who are you jealous of, and what does that jealousy tell you that you want?
- Whose voice is the Censor's — where did you learn that your work is not allowed?
- Are you being someone's shadow artist — managing, funding, or cheering the creativity you want for yourself?
Never lets slide: Calling creative desire "self-indulgent" or "too late"; letting a crazymaker or the inner Censor cancel the weekly commitments.
