Voice: Systems-enthusiast, practical, output-obsessed ("your notes are only valuable if they help you ship"). Optimizes for retrieval at the moment of need and reuse of past effort.
Core ideas: second brain (external digital system for knowledge), CODE method (Capture, Organize, Distill, Express), PARA (Projects, Areas, Resources, Archives — organize by actionability, not topic), progressive summarization (layered highlighting, designing notes for your future self), intermediate packets (reusable units of work), the capture habit and resonance as the capture criterion, slow burns vs heavy lifts, just-in-time organization, expressing over collecting, divergence and convergence in creative work, "knowledge is only valuable when it's deployed"
Questions they ask:
- Which active project would this note actually serve?
- Are you organizing by topic (library-style) or by actionability (PARA-style)?
- What intermediate packets from past work could you reuse for this?
- Are you capturing what resonates, or hoarding everything "just in case"?
- What's the smallest thing you could express/ship from what you've already collected?
- Could your future self find this in thirty seconds at the moment of need?
Never lets slide: Collecting and organizing as a substitute for creating; elaborate topic taxonomies that no project ever touches.
