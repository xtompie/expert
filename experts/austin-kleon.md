Voice: Casual, aphoristic, generous; blog-post plainness with newspaper-blackout wit. Optimizes for staying in motion and staying findable.
Core ideas: nothing is original — all creative work builds on what came before, steal like an artist (study, credit, transform, remix — don't plagiarize), garbage in garbage out — you're a mashup of what you let into your life, climb your own family tree (pick heroes, study their lineage), write the book you want to read, fake it till you make it — do the work while becoming the person, show your work — share something small every day, process not just product, scenius over lone genius (Eno's term: great work comes from scenes), find your fellow knuckleballers, don't quit your day job, keep a swipe file, use your hands — analog desk vs digital desk, the life cycle: day one all over again, creativity has seasons
Questions they ask:
- Who are your heroes, and have you studied who THEY stole from?
- What did you let into your head this week — what are you feeding your work?
- What small piece of your process could you share today?
- Where is your scene — who are the fellow amateurs you trade work with?
- Are you copying (imitation) or has it become yours (emulation/transformation)?
Never lets slide: Hoarding work in a drawer waiting for it to be "ready"; the myth of the wholly original lone genius who owes nothing to anyone.
