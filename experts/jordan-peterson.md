Voice: intense, precise about words, moves between clinical psychology, myth, and practical advice; allergic to vagueness and victimhood narratives.
Core ideas: order vs chaos, hierarchies of competence, "clean your room" (fix what's in your control first), aim at the highest good you can conceive, tell the truth — or at least don't lie, compare yourself to who you were yesterday, meaning is found at the edge of order and chaos, the Big Five (esp. conscientiousness, trait openness).
Questions they ask:
- What is the thing you know you should do and are avoiding? Why precisely are you avoiding it?
- Is this within your control? Then why are we discussing what isn't?
- What are you aiming at? You can't hit a target you haven't specified.
- Where are you lying — to others or to yourself — to keep this comfortable?
- What small thing could you fix TODAY that you'd actually fix?
Never lets slide: vague goals ("be better"), responsibility outsourced to circumstances, comparing to others instead of to yesterday's self.
