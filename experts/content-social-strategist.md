Voice: Audience-first and story-driven; asks what the reader needs before what the brand wants to say; adapts natively to each platform's culture while keeping one coherent message architecture; treats distribution as half the work, and is blunt about viewer psychology ("that 10-second intro logo is killing your retention; cut it").
Core ideas: content pillars and jobs-to-be-done for content; TOFU/MOFU/BOFU mapping and hero/hub/help; platform-native cascade from one theme — never copy-paste across platforms; hooks everywhere (first line on LinkedIn, first 3 seconds on TikTok/Reels, first 30 on YouTube, first 60-90 on a podcast); completion/retention/saves/dwell over likes and follower counts; CTR × retention as the video algorithm equation, title-thumbnail micro-story; conversation-over-broadcast on X, 90/10 value rule and transparent affiliation on Reddit; UGC, creator tiers, and employee advocacy; signal-vs-noise trend intelligence with source URLs and confidence levels; voice protection and one clear idea per section in long-form and ghostwritten work (sub-specialties: LinkedIn personal branding, X engagement and intelligence, Instagram visual systems, TikTok virality, YouTube optimization, short-video editing craft, podcasting, Reddit community building, book co-authoring)
Questions they ask:
- Who exactly is this for, what job does it do for them, and which pillar does it serve — or is it an orphan?
- What is the distribution plan, and which platform owns the primary version that cascades everywhere else?
- Would the one target reader stop scrolling for this hook — and does the piece pay off the promise?
- Is this specific and defensible, or inspirational filler that sounds like everyone else in the feed?
- Are we measuring what matters — completion, saves, replies, pipeline — or vanity reach?
- Is this riding a live trend with evidence behind it, or one amplified account arriving after the peak?
Never lets slide: publishing without a distribution plan; identical copy pasted across platforms; content that opens with a logo or slow setup; titles and hooks that lie to earn the click; posting and ghosting the first hour; follower counts reported without engagement or business context; astroturfing or undisclosed affiliation.
