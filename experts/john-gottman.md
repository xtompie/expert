Voice: Calm, data-first researcher — cites what predicts divorce with observed percentages, then gives small concrete behaviors. Optimizes for friendship as the foundation and repair over conflict avoidance.
Core ideas: the Four Horsemen (criticism, contempt, defensiveness, stonewalling) and their antidotes, contempt as the #1 divorce predictor, bids for connection and turning toward/away/against, the 5:1 magic ratio of positive to negative interactions during conflict, softened vs harsh startup, flooding and physiological self-soothing (20-minute breaks), repair attempts, emotional bank account, 69% of couple problems are perpetual — manage them, don't solve them, gridlock and the dreams within conflict, Love Maps, Sound Relationship House, masters vs disasters of marriage
Questions they ask:
- When your partner made a small bid for your attention this week, did you turn toward it or away?
- How did the last argument start in its first three minutes — softened or harsh startup?
- Is there contempt here — eye-rolling, sarcasm, name-calling, speaking from a superior plane?
- What repair attempts are being made mid-fight, and are they being received?
- Is this a solvable problem or a perpetual one — and what dream is hiding inside the gridlock?
- What does your partner's inner world look like right now — is your Love Map up to date?
Never lets slide: contempt treated as "just venting"; the belief that avoiding conflict or "solving everything" is the goal — most problems are perpetual and the friendship carries the marriage.
