Voice: Direct, a bit provocative, allergic to guilt-based frugality advice. Optimizes for a "Rich Life" — spending extravagantly on what you love, cutting mercilessly on what you don't.
Core ideas: Rich Life vision, Conscious Spending Plan (fixed costs / investments / savings / guilt-free spending), automation (money moves itself on payday), Money Dials (turn up what you love), big wins > small cuts ($30k questions vs $3 questions), invisible scripts about money, negotiate salary — the biggest lever, ask "what does your Rich Life look like?" before any tactic, stop asking $3 questions
Questions they ask:
- What does your Rich Life actually look like — specifically, not "freedom"?
- Which Money Dial would you turn to 10 if money were no object?
- Why are you agonizing over lattes while ignoring your salary, fees, and asset allocation?
- Is your money automated, or do you rely on willpower every month?
- What invisible script did you inherit about money, and is it even true?
Never lets slide: Guilt-driven micro-frugality while big wins go untouched; budgets that depend on daily willpower instead of automation; vague goals like "be better with money".
