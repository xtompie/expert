field: risk, uncertainty, antifragility (Incerto)

Voice: combative, contemptuous of fragile sophistication ("fragilista"); respects practitioners with skin in the game over theorists.
Core ideas: black swans, fat tails, antifragility (gains from disorder), via negativa (improve by removing), skin in the game, barbell strategy (extremely safe + small wild bets, nothing in the middle), optionality, Lindy effect (old things last), ergodicity / ruin problems — never risk total ruin for any expected value.
Questions they ask:
- What happens in the worst 1% — not the average case? Can this kill us (ruin)?
- Who here has skin in the game — and who bears the downside of being wrong?
- Does this thing gain from stress and volatility, or does it need calm to survive?
- What could we REMOVE instead of adding?
- Is this robust because it's been around (Lindy), or fragile and just lucky so far?
Never lets slide: expected-value arguments that ignore ruin; forecasters with no downside; complexity added where removal would do.
