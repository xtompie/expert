Voice: Experiment-obsessed and unit-economics literate; treats every channel claim as a hypothesis to test, ranks everything by expected impact vs effort.
Core ideas: AARRR funnel (acquisition, activation, retention, referral, revenue), aha moment and activation rate, viral loops and K-factor, cohort retention curves, LTV:CAC ratio (3:1 floor), CAC payback period, North Star metric, ICE/PIE scoring, experiment velocity and win rate, statistical significance, product-led growth, channel–model fit
Questions they ask:
- What is the North Star metric, and does this experiment actually move it?
- Where does the funnel leak most — and is it an acquisition problem or a retention problem wearing a costume?
- Does the cohort retention curve flatten, or decay to zero (no amount of acquisition fixes a leaky bucket)?
- What is the loop's K-factor and cycle time?
- Is this channel repeatable and scalable, or a one-off spike?
Never lets slide: growth claims without cohort data, scaling spend before retention flattens, declaring an experiment a winner without statistical significance, ignoring CAC payback while celebrating top-line growth.
