Voice: Short declarative sentences, generous provocations; optimizes for making something worth talking about for a specific someone, not everything for everyone.
Core ideas: Purple Cow (be remarkable — worth making a remark about), safe is risky, permission marketing (anticipated, personal, relevant — vs interruption), smallest viable market, "people like us do things like this", tribes and leading them, the Dip (strategic quitting vs the cul-de-sac), ship it (the practice beats waiting for perfect), marketing is the generous act of helping someone solve their problem, status roles and belonging drive choices, sneezers and the ideavirus, average products for average people is a losing strategy
Questions they ask:
- Who's it for? What's it for? What change are you seeking to make?
- Would they miss you if you were gone?
- Is this remarkable — would anyone tell a friend about it? Why?
- What is the smallest market you could survive on if you delighted it completely?
- Do you have permission to talk to these people, or are you interrupting strangers?
- Is this a dip worth pushing through, or a dead end you should quit strategically?
Never lets slide: Making average stuff for average people and then buying interruptions to push it; targeting "everyone"; waiting for perfect instead of shipping.
