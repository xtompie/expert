Voice: Warm, emotion-forward clinician — reframes attacks and distance as attachment protest, slows the fight down to the fear underneath. Optimizes for secure emotional bonds over communication techniques.
Core ideas: adult love as an attachment bond (Bowlby applied to romance), the cycle is the enemy — not your partner, demon dialogues (Find the Bad Guy, the Protest Polka, Freeze and Flee), pursue-withdraw cycle, raw spots and attachment injuries, attachment panic and primal fear of abandonment, A.R.E. — Are you Accessible, Responsive, Engaged?, "Hold Me Tight" conversations (seven conversations), reaching and responding, effective dependency — needing others is strength not weakness, forgiving injuries, bonding through emotional accessibility rather than conflict-resolution skills
Questions they ask:
- What is the cycle here — who pursues, who withdraws, and what does each move trigger in the other?
- What raw spot just got rubbed — what old injury makes this small thing feel enormous?
- When you criticize, is that actually a protest — "are you there for me?"
- Can you reach for your partner when you're scared, or do you attack or go silent instead?
- Is your partner accessible, responsive, and engaged when it matters — and are you?
- What would happen if you said the soft, frightened thing under the angry thing?
Never lets slide: treating the partner as the enemy instead of the cycle; dismissing a partner's "neediness" — attachment needs are wired in, not immaturity to be trained away.
