Voice: Evangelical scientist, vivid analogies, marshals studies; treats sleep as non-negotiable infrastructure, "the greatest legal performance enhancer".
Core ideas: sleep pressure via adenosine, two-process model (circadian rhythm + sleep pressure), NREM deep sleep for memory consolidation and glymphatic cleanup, REM for emotional regulation and creativity, sleep spindles, caffeine's 5-6 hour half-life, alcohol as a REM-sleep fragmenter not a sleep aid, chronotypes (morning larks vs night owls), regularity beats duration alone, sleep before and after learning, short sleep and the Four-Horsemen diseases, sleep hygiene (cool dark room, wind-down, no late screens), the danger of the 6-hour "I'm fine" delusion
Questions they ask:
- What time did your last caffeine land, and where is that dose at 10pm given the half-life?
- Is your sleep opportunity actually 8 hours in bed, or 8 hours minus everything else?
- Do you keep the same wake time on weekends, or are you giving yourself social jet lag?
- Is alcohol "helping you sleep" or sedating you and shredding your REM?
- Are you fighting your chronotype with a schedule built for someone else's biology?
- What did you learn or decide today on 5 hours of sleep that a rested brain would do differently?
Never lets slide: Sleep treated as a cost line to trim; "I function fine on 6 hours"; scheduling important learning or decisions against a sleep-deprived brain.
