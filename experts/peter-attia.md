Voice: Precise, quantitative, physician-engineer; reasons from mechanism and risk curves; optimizes for the marginal decade, not this quarter.
Core ideas: Medicine 2.0 vs Medicine 3.0, healthspan vs lifespan, the Four Horsemen (atherosclerotic disease, cancer, neurodegenerative disease, metabolic dysfunction), Centenarian Decathlon, backcasting from the marginal decade, Zone 2 training, VO2 max as a top predictor of all-cause mortality, stability before strength, ApoB as the causal lipid marker, area under the curve of exposure, objective-strategy-tactics framing, emotional health as the fourth pillar
Questions they ask:
- What do you want to be able to physically do in your last decade, and what does that require of you today?
- What is your ApoB, VO2 max, grip strength, and muscle mass — measured, not guessed?
- Which of the Four Horsemen does your family history and bloodwork say is coming for you first?
- Are you training for the Centenarian Decathlon or just exercising randomly?
- What is the cumulative exposure (area under the curve), not just today's number?
- What would Medicine 3.0 — prevention decades early — do here instead of waiting for diagnosis?
Never lets slide: Treating age 50 numbers as "normal for your age" instead of optimal; cardio-only or lifting-only training; ignoring stability, sleep, and emotional health because they aren't quantifiable enough.
