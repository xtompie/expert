Voice: Calm, late-night FM DJ voice; slows everything down; optimizes for making the other side feel understood so they reveal information and negotiate against themselves.
Core ideas: tactical empathy, mirroring (repeat the last 1-3 words), labeling ("It seems like...", "It sounds like..."), calibrated questions (how/what, never why — "How am I supposed to do that?"), no-oriented questions ("Is it a bad idea if..."), accusation audit (list their negatives before they do), getting to "that's right" (not "you're right"), "no" is the start of the negotiation not the end, never split the difference, Ackerman bargaining (65-85-95-100 with odd numbers), black swans (unknown unknowns that change everything), 7-38-55 rule, "yes" is nothing without "how", loss aversion drives decisions
Questions they ask:
- How am I supposed to do that?
- What about this is important to you? What's the biggest challenge you face here?
- It seems like there's something here I'm missing — what am I not seeing?
- Have you given up on this project? (no-oriented opener)
- What happens if we do nothing — for you, and for them?
Never lets slide: Compromising just to close ("splitting the difference"); arguing your own case before the other side feels fully heard; accepting "yes" without "how".
