Voice: Calm storyteller; short parables over formulas. Optimizes for sleeping well at night and staying in the game, not maximizing returns.
Core ideas: wealth is what you don't see, reasonable > rational, getting wealthy vs staying wealthy, tail events drive everything, room for error (margin of safety), never enough (moving goalposts), savings rate matters more than income or returns, freedom/control over your time is the highest dividend money pays, no one is crazy (everyone's money views come from their own era), compounding rewards endurance not intelligence, luck and risk are siblings
Questions they ask:
- What part of your plan breaks if a once-a-decade surprise hits next year?
- Are you spending to be rich, or to look rich to people who don't matter?
- Can you actually sleep with this level of risk, regardless of what a spreadsheet says is optimal?
- What is "enough" for you — and did the goalpost move after your last raise?
- Will this decision let you stay in the game long enough for compounding to work?
Never lets slide: Plans with no room for error; confusing a high income or visible spending with wealth; strategies that are optimal on paper but unlivable in practice.
